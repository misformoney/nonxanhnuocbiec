Ha Long Bay

Quang Ninh Province

Featuring over 1,900 islands and caves, Ha Long Bay is well-known for its majestic beauty. The name "Ha Long" stems from legends of dragons descending to protect the Vietnamese realm. This natural wonder has earned UNESCO World Heritage status twice in 1994 and 2000.

Cò Lả

Northen Folk Songs / Dân Ca Bắc Bộ

A genre of Vietnamese traditional music, originating from agricultural life in the Red River Delta. With gentle melodies, they are sung during festivals or while laboring.
Co La uses call-and-response lyrics along with metaphors like peaches, plums, betel, and areca to express love and longing for union. Northern Delta’s rustic beauty is also captured via the stork.

DONG XAM TEMPLE

Thai Binh Province

Located by Vong River, Dong Xam Temple spans 10,000m² with intricate architecture. Its standout characteristic is Thuy Toa building in the center a pond, showcasing harmony between humans and nature. Dong Xam festival (April 1-3, lunar calendar) is celebrated annually to honor traditional values. In 1990, the temple was recognized as a national cultural heritage site.

Đào Liễu

Cheo Singing / Hát Chèo

Cheo Singing - a traditional Vietnamese theatrical art which blends singing, acting, and dancing. Founded by Pham Thi Tran in the 10th century, Cheo features witty performance style depicting history, legends, and folklore.
The willow in the song symbolizes the elegance of Northern Vietnamese women. Dao Lieu’s lyrics features call-and-response style, portraying sincere love and, more broadly, the communal spirit of rural culture.

Phong Nha Cave

Quang Binh Province

Located in Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park, this UNESCO World Heritage Site (2003) spans 7,500m with 12 main chambers and interconnecting branches. The name Phong Nha refers to stalactites resembling "wind through teeth”. Inside the cave, there are underground rivers, sandbanks, and distinctive stalactite formations similar to lion, unicorn and elephant, etc.

Lý Mười Thương

Hue Singing / Ca Huế

Hue Singing - a traditional music genre from Hue, emerged in the 17th century and blending courtly elegance with folk simplicity. It revolves around the cultural life of the ancient capital, performed by singers with traditional instruments like zither and moon lute.
This song is a sweet love confession from a men to the woman he loves. Ten things he admires, from her outer beauty to her inner charm, reveal the perfect virtues of a central Vietnamese woman: gentle and modest.

Thien Mu Pagoda

Thua Thien Hue Province

Thien Mu Pagoda, gracefully situated by Huong River, features the iconic Phuoc Duyen Tower, rising 21m across seven stories. Built in 1601 during the Nguyen lords' prosperous era, it stands as a revered spiritual symbol of Hue, beautifully reflecting the evolution of Vietnam's Buddhist and architectural heritage through the centuries.

Hò Giã Gạo

Chanty / Hò

Ho - a branch of Binh Tri Thien folk songs that accompanies collective rice pounding. Performed as call-and-response between male and female, hò features rhythmic, intense beats and unfolds in three stages: invitation, engagement, and farewell.
Ho Gia Gao praises unity and joy in communal labor. The call-and-response lyrics joyfully highlight the performers' quick wit and remarkable flexibility in communication.

Xiem Can Pagoda

Bac Lieu Province

One of the largest religious complexes in the Mekong Delta, the pagoda features Angkor-style architecture with symbols like multi-headed snakes, dancing maidens, and Krut. Today, it serves as a cultural and spiritual hub for Khmer people, preserving their heritage through Khmer language and Theravada Buddhist teachings.

| SONG

Dạ Cổ Hoài Lang

Vong Co / Vọng Cổ

As a traditional music genre of Southern culture, vong co traces its roots to the piece “Da Co Hoai Lang”, composed by Cao Van Lay in 1919. It has evolved into an indispenable part of cai luong and is often accompanied by traditional instruments like moon lute, zither, monochord and lute.
Da Co Hoai Lang portrays a wife's yearning for husband while hearing the sound of night drum. Through imagery of the moon, swallows and night drum, this piece reflects Vietnamese loyalty in love and concerns of socio-political circumstances back then.

| LOCATION

Tra Su Forest

An Giang Province

Tra Su Forest is a wetland ecosystem located in the west of the Hau River. Famous not only for its vast melaleuca fields and interwoven canals, but also as home to numerous water birds, unique wildlife, and aquatic species. With its diverse ecosystem, the forest is often likened to a beautiful natural painting in verdant hues. Local people typically use small, traditional boats to travel along the forest.

Lý Chim Quyên

Souther Ly / Lý Nam Bộ

Southern ly - a popular folk melody across Vietnam, differs from hò through its fixed musical structure and steady rhythm. People perform this genre either as a solo or in call-and-response style, accompanied by traditional instruments, its content captures daily life and nature.
Ly Chim Quyen mentions cuckoo bird as a metaphor to represents deep love for homeland and aspirations for prosperity. The song portrays the region's minimal way of life and its people's sincere emotions.
